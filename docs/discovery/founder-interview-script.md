# Founder Interview Script
# نص مقابلة المؤسسين

> Bilingual script for discovery interviews with Saudi startup founders. Use this verbatim or adapt naturally — the questions are designed to surface real pain, not to be a checklist. Take notes during; do not record without explicit consent.
>
> **Time budget:** 30–45 minutes per interview. If they want to give you more, take it. If they're rushed, hit Q1, Q3, Q5, Q9, Q14.
>
> **Goal:** find out whether Saudi founders have a real, painful, monetizable problem managing their cap table — and whether our wedge (Saudi-native, Companies Law 2023-aware) actually matters to them.

---

## Pre-interview prep

### Who to interview
- Saudi-domiciled startup founders (LLC, SJSC, or transitioning JSC)
- Mix of stages: pre-seed, seed, Series A, possibly a Series B for contrast
- Mix of sectors: don't over-index on fintech
- Use the team's existing network first — Aramco/EY/Deloitte/Roland Berger contacts likely know dozens

### Logistics
- 30–45 min, video call or in person
- Have water, no sales materials, no laptop screen-sharing your product (we don't have one)
- One interviewer + one note-taker if possible. If solo, take notes after, not during.
- End by asking "Who else should I talk to?" — referrals are gold.

### Mindset
- You are not selling. You are learning.
- You are not validating that you're right. You are trying to find out you're wrong.
- The best interview is the one where they tell you the problem isn't what you thought it was.

## Opening (3–5 min)

### EN

> "Thanks for taking the time. I'm working on understanding how Saudi founders manage their company's equity — cap tables, founder shares, employee stock plans, that kind of thing. I'm not selling anything. I'm trying to learn. Everything you say stays between us unless you say otherwise. Mind if I take notes?"

> "Before we start — can you give me a quick overview of your company? What you do, when you started, how many founders, where you are in fundraising?"

### AR

> "شكراً لوقتك. أنا أحاول أفهم كيف المؤسسين السعوديين يديرون ملكية شركاتهم — جدول الملكية، أسهم المؤسسين، برامج أسهم الموظفين، وأشياء من هذا النوع. ما أبيع لك شي، أنا أحاول أتعلم. كل شي تقوله يبقى بيني وبينك إلا إذا قلت غير ذلك. تسمح لي آخذ ملاحظات؟"

> "قبل ما نبدأ — ممكن تعطيني نبذة سريعة عن شركتكم؟ وش تشتغلون، متى بدأتم، كم مؤسس، وأين أنتم في رحلة التمويل؟"

## Section 1 — Current state (5 min)

### Q1. How is your cap table currently tracked?
**EN:** "How does your company keep track of who owns what? Walk me through it."
**AR:** "كيف شركتكم تتابع من يملك ماذا في الشركة؟ خذني خطوة بخطوة."

> *Listen for: spreadsheet (most common), Notion/docs, lawyer's file, Carta/EquityList, "we don't really track it." The answer reveals everything.*

### Q2. Who in the company is responsible for it?
**EN:** "Who actually owns this? Like, if I needed to know your cap table right now, who would I ask?"
**AR:** "من المسؤول عن هذا الموضوع فعلياً؟ يعني لو احتجت أعرف جدول الملكية الآن، لمن أرجع؟"

> *Listen for: founder/CEO themselves (red flag — too senior to be doing this), CFO, external lawyer, "nobody really" (huge red flag — and big opportunity).*

### Q3. When was the last time you needed to update it, and why?
**EN:** "When was the last time you actually had to update the cap table? What happened?"
**AR:** "متى آخر مرة احتجتم تحدّثون جدول الملكية؟ وش اللي صار؟"

> *Listen for: funding round, hire with equity, partner exit, AoA amendment. The frequency tells you the pain frequency.*

---

## Section 2 — Pain points (10 min)

### Q4. Walk me through your last fundraising round from a paperwork/cap table perspective. What was hardest?
**EN:** "Take me back to your last fundraising round. From the moment you signed the term sheet to the moment money hit the account — what was the most painful part of the cap table / paperwork side of it?"
**AR:** "خذني للجولة التمويلية الأخيرة. من لحظة توقيعكم على ورقة الشروط إلى لحظة دخول المبلغ في الحساب — وش أصعب جزء واجهتوه في موضوع جدول الملكية والأوراق؟"

> *This is the headline question. Listen for: lawyer back-and-forth, AoA amendments, ZATCA, MoC, "we discovered our cap table was wrong," "it took 3 months," "we paid a lawyer X SAR for what felt like a glorified Excel."*

### Q5. Have you ever had a disagreement or confusion among founders or with investors about who owns what?
**EN:** "Has there ever been a moment where you and a co-founder, or you and an investor, weren't on the same page about ownership percentages or terms?"
**AR:** "هل صار في وقت ما، أنت وأحد الشركاء أو أحد المستثمرين، ما كنتم متفقين على نسب الملكية أو على الشروط؟"

> *Sensitive question. Phrase carefully. Listen for: dilution math errors, vesting confusion, "we found out later that X was wrong."*

### Q6. Do you have an ESOP, and if so, how do you administer it?
**EN:** "Do you have any kind of employee stock or equity plan? If yes, how do you handle vesting, departures, exercises?"
**AR:** "هل عندكم برنامج لأسهم الموظفين أو أي نوع من الملكية للموظفين؟ إذا أيوه، كيف تتعاملون مع جداول الاستحقاق، خروج الموظفين، تنفيذ الخيارات؟"

> *Listen for: "we don't have one yet but we want one," "we have one but it's a mess," "our lawyer handles it." All three are opportunities.*

### Q7. How do you think about Saudi-specific legal requirements? Has the new Companies Law affected you?
**EN:** "Since the new Companies Law came in early 2023, has anything changed for you in how you manage your company structure or equity?"
**AR:** "من بعد ما صدر نظام الشركات الجديد بداية 2023، هل تغير شي عندكم في طريقة إدارة هيكل الشركة أو الملكية؟"

> *Listen for: AoA amendment work, SJSC consideration, ESOP enabling, awareness level. If they don't know there's a new law — that's a discovery in itself.*

---

## Section 3 — Tools and money (10 min)

### Q8. Have you ever used Carta, EquityList, Qapita, or any cap table software?
**EN:** "Have you tried any cap table software? Carta, EquityList, Qapita, anything else?"
**AR:** "هل جربتم أي برنامج لإدارة جدول الملكية؟ زي Carta أو EquityList أو Qapita أو غيرها؟"

> *Listen for: "we tried it but...", "our investors use it but...", "I've heard of it but...", "what's that?"*

### Q9. (If yes) What works and what doesn't?
**EN:** "What did you like? What was missing or wrong for a Saudi company?"
**AR:** "وش أعجبكم فيه؟ وش كان ناقص أو غلط بالنسبة لشركة سعودية؟"

> *Headline question for our positioning. Listen for: "they don't understand SJSC," "everything was in dollars," "ESOP templates were US-style," "our lawyer told us it wasn't compliant."*

### Q10. (If no) Why not?
**EN:** "Why haven't you tried any of those tools?"
**AR:** "ليش ما جربتم أي من هذي الأدوات؟"

> *Listen for: "didn't know they existed," "too expensive," "didn't seem worth it," "lawyer said spreadsheet is fine."*

### Q11. Roughly, how much do you spend per year on lawyer fees related to corporate / equity work?
**EN:** "Ballpark — how much have you spent in the last 12 months on lawyer fees for corporate or equity-related work? Drafting, amendments, MoC stuff, that kind of thing."
**AR:** "تقريباً — كم صرفتم على المحامي في آخر 12 شهر في موضوع الشركة وهيكل الملكية؟ صياغة عقود، تعديلات، أشياء وزارة التجارة، إلى آخره."

> *Listen for: range. SAR 5K? 50K? 500K? This is the alternative spend our product partially displaces.*

### Q12. If a tool existed that handled this for Saudi companies specifically, what would you pay?
**EN:** "Hypothetically — if a tool existed that handled all of this, built specifically for Saudi companies, what would feel like a fair price per month or per year? I'm not pitching anything, I'm just trying to calibrate."
**AR:** "افتراضياً — لو في أداة موجودة تعالج كل هذا، ومصممة خصيصاً للشركات السعودية، كم سعرها الشهري أو السنوي يكون مناسب من وجهة نظرك؟ مو أبيع، أحاول أقدّر السعر."

> *Listen for: hesitation (means they don't see the value), confident answer (means they have a benchmark), surprisingly high (means we're solving real pain), "free" (means we haven't found the pain).*

---

## Section 4 — Forward-looking (5 min)

### Q13. What's coming up in the next 12 months that involves your cap table?
**EN:** "Looking at the next 12 months — fundraising, hires with equity, possible exits — what's coming up that touches the cap table?"
**AR:** "بالنظر للسنة القادمة — تمويل، توظيف بأسهم، احتمال خروج — وش الأشياء القادمة اللي لها علاقة بجدول الملكية؟"

> *Tells you when their next pain spike is. Useful for sales timing if they're a future customer.*

### Q14. If you could wave a magic wand and have one thing solved in this whole area, what would it be?
**EN:** "If you could wave a magic wand and have one thing solved — one specific thing — in everything we've been talking about, what would it be?"
**AR:** "لو عندك عصا سحرية وممكن تحل شي واحد — شي محدد — في كل اللي تكلمنا عنه، وش يكون؟"

> *The "magic wand" question. Often surfaces the actual #1 pain better than direct asking.*

---

## Section 5 — Wrap (5 min)

### Q15. Who else should I talk to?
**EN:** "Three more questions. First — who else should I talk to? Other founders dealing with similar things who'd have useful perspective?"
**AR:** "ثلاثة أسئلة أخيرة. الأول — مين غيرك ممكن أتكلم معه؟ مؤسسين ثانيين عندهم نفس التحديات وممكن يفيدوني؟"

> *Get names. Get warm intros if possible.*

### Q16. Is there anything I should have asked but didn't?
**EN:** "Second — is there anything in this area you'd have expected me to ask, that I didn't? Anything you wish I'd brought up?"
**AR:** "الثاني — هل في شي في هذا المجال كان مفروض أسأل عنه وما سألت؟ شي تتمنى أنا فتحت موضوعه؟"

> *Critical. The thing they bring up here is often the most important thing in the entire interview.*

### Q17. Can I follow up?
**EN:** "Last — if I make progress on this, would it be okay to come back to you in a few weeks? Both to share what I've learned and to test specific ideas?"
**AR:** "الأخير — لو طوّرنا الموضوع، أقدر أرجع لك بعد أسابيع؟ عشان أشاركك اللي تعلمته وأختبر معك أفكار معينة؟"

> *Permission to recontact = gold. Most will say yes if the interview was good.*

## Post-interview ritual (within 30 minutes)

While it's fresh:

1. **Write up notes immediately.** What they said in their own words, not your interpretation.
2. **Tag the interview** in the Notion `Interviews` database with key fields: stage, sector, current tooling, willingness to pay range, top pain.
3. **Capture surprises.** What did you assume that turned out wrong? What did they bring up that you hadn't considered?
4. **Record their referrals** as new entries in the `Interview Pipeline` database.
5. **Send a thank-you note within 24 hours.** Reference one specific thing they said. Not a templated message.

---

## Synthesis after 5+ interviews

Don't try to synthesize after 1 or 2 — the patterns aren't there yet. After 5, you'll start to see:

- The **dominant tooling** (probably spreadsheets)
- The **dominant pain** (probably one of: fundraising chaos, ESOP confusion, lawyer-cost frustration, AoA amendment headaches, or "I don't think about it until I have to")
- The **price tolerance band** (probably 200–2000 SAR/month range, with outliers)
- The **competitive awareness** (probably low — most founders haven't seriously evaluated Carta/EquityList)
- The **wedge resonance** (does "Saudi-native" actually matter, or do they just want any solution?)

Synthesize into the Notion `Discovery Findings` page after every 3 interviews. Don't wait until the end.

---

## What "validation" looks like

The discovery sprint passes if **at least 6 of 10 interviews** show:

- A real, articulated pain in cap table management (not "yeah I guess it's annoying")
- Active spend on alternatives (lawyer time, software, internal hours)
- Specific dissatisfaction with current state
- Willingness to pay something for a solution (number, even if low)
- At least 3 of those 6 mention Saudi-specific issues unprompted

If you don't hit those, the wedge isn't there. Pivot or kill — see ADR-0002 (to be written based on findings).

If you blow past those, accelerate. The market is real.
